Make Flow of Work Visible to Team and Customer

Provide real-time visibility into status of work items (user stories, defects, tasks) throughout the software lifecycle to help prioritise, track progress and improve communication.

Key aspects

  • Kanban/SCRUM Boards: Use boards with columns like "To Do", "In Progress", and "Completed"; move cards as work progresses.
  • Task Tracking Tools: Centralize tasks in tools like Jira, Trello, or Asana to create, assign and track work items.
  • Real-time Communication: Daily stand-ups, huddles and chat tools (Slack, Teams) keep stakeholders updated and surface blockers quickly.
  • Information Radiators: Use physical or digital boards, burn-down charts and KPIs in shared spaces so everyone can see status at a glance.
  • Regular Demonstrations & Reviews: Demo completed work to customers frequently to gather feedback and align expectations.
  • Feedback Loops: Collect and act on customer and team feedback to continuously improve the workflow and product.

PHR Guidelines / Work Instructions

MFVCG-1 — Establish Clear Communication Channels [ISO]

  1. Set up regular stand-up meetings or huddles to discuss progress and issues.
  2. Use chat tools like Slack or Microsoft Teams for real-time collaboration.
  3. Maintain an open approach for stakeholders to ask questions or clarify requirements.

Related risks: MFVCR-1, MFVCR-6

MFVCG-2 — Implement Kanban/SCRUM Boards [ISO]

  1. Create columns for workflow stages (To Do, In Progress, Done).
  2. Add tasks as cards and move them as work progresses.
  3. Regularly review and update the board with the team and customer.

Related risks: MFVCR-2, MFVCR-4

MFVCG-3 — Use Task Tracking Tools [ISO]

  1. Select a tracking tool (e.g., Jira, Trello, Asana).
  2. Create and assign tasks; keep statuses up to date.
  3. Use dashboards and filters to provide stakeholders with relevant views.

Related risks: MFVCR-3, MFVCR-8

MFVCG-4 — Hold Regular Demonstrations and Reviews

  1. Schedule regular demos with customers to present completed work.
  2. Collect feedback and incorporate it into future iterations.
  3. Use demo outcomes to realign priorities where necessary.

Related risks: MFVCR-7

MFVCG-5 — Display Information Radiators

  1. Decide what metrics and views are most useful (e.g., timelines, burn-downs).
  2. Set up physical or digital displays accessible to team and customers.
  3. Keep the displays updated to reflect current status.

Related risks: MFVCR-5, MFVCR-9

MFVCG-6 — Encourage Continuous Feedback

  1. Seek feedback from customers on completed work and address concerns.
  2. Run retrospectives to identify process improvements.
  3. Apply changes based on feedback to improve delivery and quality.

Related risks: MFVCR-10

MFVCG-7 — Ensure Traceability from Requirement to Committed Code [ISO 4.6.1]

  1. Capture user stories in a requirements tool and assign identifiers.
  2. Reference story identifiers in VCS commit messages.
  3. Maintain a traceability matrix mapping stories to commits.

Related risks: MFVCR-1, MFVCR-7

MFVCG-8 — Ensure Comprehensive Bug/Defect Reporting [ISO 4.8]

  1. Document failure, expected behaviour, test data and conditions.
  2. Provide environment and build details to help reproduce issues.
  3. Prioritise and triage defects promptly.

Related risks: MFVCR-2, MFVCR-10

MFVCG-9 — Identify and Communicate Blocked Features and Dependencies [ISO 4.2.7]

  1. Regularly review and flag blocked or delayed features.
  2. Document dependencies and blockers and communicate them clearly.
  3. Arrange stakeholder discussions to resolve blockers quickly.

Related risks: MFVCR-1, MFVCR-7, MFVCR-9

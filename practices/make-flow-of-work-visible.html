Make Flow of Work Visible to Team and Customer

Making the flow of work visible to team members and customers is the practice of providing real-time visibility into the status of work items, such as user stories, defects, or tasks, throughout the software development lifecycle. This approach enables teams to prioritize work, track progress, and communicate effectively with customers, improving overall customer satisfaction and team efficiency.

Key aspects covered by the practice

  • Kanban/SCRUM Boards: Utilizing Kanban/SCRUM boards is a popular method to visualize the flow of work. It involves dividing the software development process into distinct stages or columns, such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Completed." Each task or user story is represented as a card that moves across the columns as it progresses, making it easy to track the status of individual tasks and the overall workflow.
  • Task Tracking Tools: Employing task tracking tools, such as Jira, Trello, or Asana, enables teams to record and manage tasks efficiently. These tools provide a centralized platform where team members can create, assign, and track tasks. The visibility of task statuses helps team members and customers understand which tasks are in progress, completed, or awaiting action.
  • Real-time Communication: Effective communication is crucial for making the flow of work visible. Teams should have regular stand-up meetings or huddles to discuss progress, address any obstacles, and share updates. Additionally, using communication tools like Slack or Microsoft Teams fosters continuous collaboration and enables team members and customers to stay informed about the project's progress.
  • Information Radiators: Displaying project-related information on physical or digital boards, known as information radiators, is an excellent way to make the workflow visible to everyone involved. These boards may include project timelines, burn-down charts, task boards, or metrics on key performance indicators. By placing them in shared spaces or online platforms, teams and customers can easily access and understand the project's status.
  • Regular Demonstrations and Reviews: Conducting regular demonstrations or reviews with the customer helps maintain transparency and align expectations. These sessions provide an opportunity for the customer to observe and provide feedback on the work completed, ensuring that the project is progressing in the desired direction. By involving the customer in this process, any potential misunderstandings or misalignments can be identified and addressed early on.
  • Feedback Loops: Establishing feedback loops with the customer and within the development team is crucial for continuous improvement. By seeking feedback on completed work, teams can identify areas for enhancement, optimize their processes, and ensure they are delivering value to the customer. Open channels of communication enable the team and customer to collaborate and refine the workflow based on shared insights.

Risks of not implementing Practice

Risk NumberTitleDescription
MFVCR-1Misalignment of ExpectationsLack of visibility can lead to misunderstandings and misaligned expectations between the team and the customer.
MFVCR-2Delayed Issue IdentificationWithout visibility, issues or bottlenecks in the workflow may go unnoticed, leading to delays in project delivery.
MFVCR-3Inefficient Resource AllocationWithout a clear view of the flow of work, it becomes challenging to allocate resources effectively, resulting in underutilization or overburdening of team members.
MFVCR-4Unclear Work PrioritizationA lack of visibility makes it difficult to prioritize tasks, leading to confusion about which work items should be addressed first.
MFVCR-5Decreased AccountabilityWhen the flow of work is not visible, it becomes harder to track individual and team accountability, potentially leading to a decrease in overall performance.
MFVCR-6Limited Customer InputWithout visibility into the workflow, customers may feel disconnected from the development process and have limited opportunities to provide timely feedback.
MFVCR-7Increased Risk of Scope CreepWithout clear visibility, there is a higher risk of scope creep as changes and new requirements might not be adequately captured and tracked.
MFVCR-8Reduced Team CollaborationLack of visibility hinders effective collaboration within the team, as team members may not be aware of the progress or challenges faced by their peers.
MFVCR-9Difficulty in Identifying Workflow InefficienciesWithout visibility, it becomes harder to identify and address workflow inefficiencies or bottlenecks, potentially leading to decreased productivity.
MFVCR-10Limited Opportunities for Continuous ImprovementWithout visibility into the flow of work, it becomes challenging to identify areas for improvement and implement changes that enhance the development process.

Regularly update and review the Kanban/SCRUM board with the team and customer. MFVCR-2 - Delayed Issue Identification MFVCR-4 - Unclear Work Prioritization

Clearly state the expected behavior or outcome based on the requirements or specifications. 3. Update the project management tools, Kanban boards, or any other visual representation of the workflow to reflect the blocked features prominently. 4. Schedule regular meetings or discussions with relevant stakeholders to address the blockers and devise corrective actions.

Lightweight Change Approval

Adopt a streamlined change approval process focused on peer review and minimal overhead to improve velocity and quality.

Key aspects

  • Lightweight Process: Keep approvals efficient and avoid unnecessary bureaucracy.
  • Peer Review: Use code reviews, pair programming and intrateam reviews as primary approval mechanisms.
  • Superior Performance: Research shows lightweight approvals often outperform heavy CAB processes in IT performance.

Risks of not implementing

RiskDescription
LCAR-1Bottlenecked Decision-Making — slow approvals increase cycle time.
LCAR-2Increased Time-to-Market — long processes delay releases.
LCAR-3Decreased Agility — inability to adapt quickly to change.

Guidelines / Work Instructions

  1. LCAG-1 - Define Approval Criteria: Set clear, objective criteria for approvals.
  2. LCAG-2 - Automate Workflows: Use issue trackers and CI to automate notifications and approvals.
  3. LCAG-3 - CI & Testing: Enforce CI tests and quality gates to reduce manual checks.
  4. LCAG-4 - Foster Collaboration: Promote peer review and knowledge sharing within teams.
  5. LCAG-5 - Simplify Documentation: Keep change request forms short and focused on impact and rollback plans.

Limit Work In-Progress (WIP)

Limiting Work In-Progress (WIP) helps teams maintain focus and flow by controlling how many items are actively worked on.

Definition & Benefits

Limiting WIP involves setting a maximum number of tasks or stories that can be in progress simultaneously. Benefits include reduced multitasking, improved flow, higher throughput, better collaboration, and higher quality.

Key aspects

  • Set Realistic WIP Limits: Determine limits per workflow stage based on team capacity and historical data.
  • Reduce Multitasking: Encourage finishing work before pulling new items.
  • Visualize Work: Use Kanban boards to expose bottlenecks and blockers.
  • Pull System: Team members pull work only when capacity exists.
  • Measure & Improve: Track lead time, cycle time, and throughput to optimize limits.

Risks of not implementing WIP limits

RiskDescription
WIPR-1Overloaded Resources — risk of burnout and lower productivity.
WIPR-2Increased Lead Time — slow delivery due to work piling up.
WIPR-3Reduced Focus and Attention — lower quality due to context switching.
WIPR-4Inefficient Collaboration — fragmented team efforts.
WIPR-5Hidden Bottlenecks — inability to spot process issues early.

Guidelines / Work Instructions

  1. WIPG-1 - Set Realistic WIP Limits: Analyze capacity and set per-stage limits.
  2. WIPG-2 - Visualize WIP on a Board: Keep the board updated and review it frequently.
  3. WIPG-3 - Implement Pull System: Start new work only when capacity is available.
  4. WIPG-4 - Collaborate and Communicate: Use stand-ups and pairing to unblock work.
  5. WIPG-5 - Measure Performance: Track metrics and iterate on limits.
  6. WIPG-6 - Prioritize Based on Value: Pull higher-value work first.

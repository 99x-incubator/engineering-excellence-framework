Work in Small Batches

Working in small batches is a best practice that emphasizes breaking down tasks and delivering them incrementally.

Overview

Working in small batches is a software engineering best practice that emphasizes breaking down tasks and delivering them incrementally. It involves dividing work into small, manageable pieces and completing them in short cycles.

Key aspects covered by the practice

  • Iterative Development: Deliver in short iterations that produce potentially shippable increments.
  • Continuous Integration: Integrate frequently to detect issues early.
  • Rapid Feedback Loop: Get feedback quickly to make course corrections.
  • Risk Mitigation: Reduce large, risky changes by addressing challenges early.
  • Flexibility and Adaptability: Easier to reprioritize and adapt to changing requirements.
  • Continuous Delivery: Aim for always-releasable software.
  • Quality Focus: Emphasize testing and reviews on smaller slices of work.
  • Reduced Time to Market: Deliver incremental value faster.
  • Collaboration and Communication: Improve team coordination and knowledge sharing.

Risks of not implementing this practice

WSBR-2Increased Integration Challenges — large merges and fragile integrations.
WSBR-3Limited Adaptability — hard to change course quickly.
WSBR-4Higher Risk of Project Failure — issues not surfaced early.
WSBR-5Delayed Time to Market — bigger tasks take longer to deliver value.
WSBR-6Decreased Collaboration — silos form around large tasks.
WSBR-7Inefficient Testing and Debugging — harder to test large changes.
WSBR-8Reduced Flexibility — inability to respond to feedback quickly.
WSBR-9Lower Productivity — longer lead times and increased complexity.
WSBR-10Decreased Customer Satisfaction — slower delivery of value.

Guidelines / Work Instructions

  1. WSBG-1 - Break Work into Small Tasks: Identify larger tasks and break them down into smaller units that can be completed in a few days.
  2. WSBG-2 - Define Clear Acceptance Criteria: Collaborate with stakeholders to set unambiguous acceptance criteria (functional, non-functional, security, performance).
  3. WSBG-3 - Prioritize and Sequence Tasks: Order tasks based on dependencies and business value.
  4. WSBG-4 - Implement Continuous Integration: Set up automated builds, tests, and reviews triggered by changes.
  5. WSBG-5 - Perform Regular Code Reviews: Establish review practices to improve quality.
  6. WSBG-6 - Adopt Test-Driven Development (TDD): Write tests before features to ensure quality.
  7. WSBG-7 - Automate Build and Deployment Processes: Automate CI/CD to support frequent delivery.
  8. WSBG-8 - Encourage Pair Programming: Promote collaboration and knowledge sharing.
  9. WSBG-9 - Conduct Retrospectives and Act on Feedback: Continuously improve via retrospectives.
  10. WSBG-10 - Maintain a Prioritized and Flexible Backlog: Keep a backlog prioritized by business value and revisit it regularly.

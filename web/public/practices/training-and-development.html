Training and Development

Training and development is the practice of investing in the skills and knowledge of team members to improve their performance and effectiveness. This approach enables teams to continuously improve and adapt to changing business needs, improving overall team performance.

Key aspects covered by the practice

  • Version Control
  • Work in Small Batches
  • Limit Work In-Progress (WIP)
  • Adhering to Coding Standards
  • Trunk-Based Development
  • Loosely Coupled Architecture
  • Continuous Integration
  • Shift Left on Quality
  • Test Automation
  • Test Data Management
  • Deployment Automation
  • Shift Left on Security and Privacy
  • Lightweight Change Approval
  • Proactive Notification on Build and Deployment Process
  • Monitoring and Obtaining Feedback from Production
  • Make Flow of Work Visible to Team and Customer
  • Visual Management of Current Application Production, Build, and Deployment State
  • Empowered Teams
  • Team Experimentation
  • Continuous Delivery
  • Communication and Collaboration Skills
  • Continuous Learning and Skill Development
  • Agile Methodologies and Practices

PHR Guidelines / Work Instructions

Guideline NumberNamePurposePerforming MethodPossible Risks if Failed
TDG-1 Establish Training Needs Assessment To identify the training needs of the team and ensure targeted and effective training programs. 1. Conduct surveys and interviews to gather feedback from team members on their training needs.
2. Analyze project requirements and performance gaps to identify specific training areas.
3. Prioritize training needs based on criticality and impact on project success. 		TDR-1 - Lack of Standardization
TDR-2 - Low Awareness of Industry Trends
TDG-2 Develop Comprehensive Training Curriculum [ISO 4.11.1] To create a structured curriculum that covers all essential software engineering best practices and methodologies. 1. Identify key topics and learning objectives based on training needs assessment. Make sure to include any Project specific special knowledge areas.
2. Organize training content into modules and lessons.
3. Define the learning path and prerequisites for each module.
4. Incorporate practical exercises and hands-on projects to reinforce learning. 		TDR-1 - Lack of Standardization
TDR-3 - Inefficient Collaboration
TDG-3 Engage Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) To ensure the training program is delivered by knowledgeable experts in each subject area. 1. Identify SMEs within the organization or seek external experts if necessary.
2. Collaborate with SMEs to review and validate training content.
3. Schedule sessions or workshops where SMEs can share their expertise with the team. 		TDR-1 - Lack of Standardization
TDR-3 - Inefficient Collaboration
TDG-4 Implement Blended Learning Approach To provide a variety of learning methods and resources to accommodate different learning styles and maximize knowledge retention. 1. Combine instructor-led training sessions with online self-paced courses.
2. Incorporate interactive workshops, group discussions, and case studies.
3. Provide access to reference materials, documentation, and online resources for self-study.
4. Encourage continuous learning through webinars, conferences, and industry events. 		TDR-4 - Lack of Continuous Learning
TDR-5 - Ineffective Agile Implementation
TDG-5 Establish Mentorship Programs To facilitate knowledge transfer and provide guidance and support to less experienced team members. 1. Pair experienced team members with junior members for mentorship.
2. Define mentorship goals and expectations.
3. Encourage regular knowledge-sharing sessions and feedback discussions.
4. Monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the mentorship program. 		TDR-3 - Inefficient Collaboration
TDR-6 - Increased Technical Debt
TDG-6 Allocate Dedicated Time for Learning To ensure team members have dedicated time and resources for continuous learning and skill development. 1. Allocate a specific portion of work hours each week for learning activities.
2. Provide access to learning platforms and online courses.
3. Encourage participation in relevant workshops and conferences.
4. Recognize and reward team members for their learning achievements. 		TDR-4 - Lack of Continuous Learning
TDR-6 - Increased Technical Debt
TDG-7 Measure Training Effectiveness To assess the impact and effectiveness of the training program and make necessary improvements. 1. Conduct pre-training and post-training assessments to measure knowledge gain.
2. Collect feedback from participants through surveys or interviews.
3. Monitor the application of learned skills in real-world projects.
4. Use key performance indicators (KPIs) to track improvements in project delivery and quality. 		TDR-7 - Limited Adoption of Continuous Integration and Delivery
TDR-10 - Lack of Visibility and Monitoring
